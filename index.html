          Article

          Starting a conversation can be easier than you think. Focus on common ground, open-ended questions, and a kind tone.

          3 Practical Openers

          1. Common Interest: “I noticed you like [topic], what’s your latest find?”
          2. Light Compliment: “Your taste in [music/film] is great, any recent favorites?”
          3. Context Hook: “Saw your story about [event], how was it?”

          Tip: Keep it specific, friendly, and easy to answer.

          When replies slow down, don’t panic. Give space, reset tone, and re-engage with low-pressure prompts.

          Recovery Steps

          1. Wait a bit: Avoid double texting too fast.
          2. Re-open softly: “Hey! Just saw [topic], made me think of you.”
          3. Offer easy choices: “Coffee or a short walk this week?”

          Avoid long paragraphs or pressure. Keep it warm and simple.
